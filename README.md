## Part V · The Perovskite Optical Synapse and STOKES Cross-Substrate Universality

### V.1 The Result

A multidimensional and reconfigurable optical neuromorphic computing system using perovskite-based all-photonic synapses was reported in Communications Materials (2026). The system demonstrates that all-optical neuromorphic computing can be realized on perovskite substrates, extending the platform beyond rare-earth-doped phosphors.

The perovskite optical synapse introduces a qualitatively different material class. Where the STOKES Yan et al. device (Advanced Photonics, 2026) used a rare-earth-doped long-afterglow crystal (SrAl₂O₄:Eu²⁺,Dy³⁺ family), the perovskite device uses a halide perovskite structure — materials with the ABX₃ crystal architecture (A = organic or inorganic cation, B = metal cation, X = halide anion) that have revolutionized solar cell efficiency and LED technology.

In the perovskite optical synapse, the rol of the rare-earth trap states is played by:

- **Polaron trapping**: mobile charge carriers in the perovskite lattice become localized polarons (electron + lattice distortion) at structural defects, with trapping and release timescales in the milliseconds-to-seconds range — directly analogous to the rare-earth 4f electron trap.
- **Ion migration**: halide vacancies migrate under optical excitation, redistributing the local electric field and modulating the emission; this provides a **second** trap timescale (seconds to minutes) distinct from the polaron timescale.
- **Reconfigurability**: unlike the fixed trap-depth distribution of rare-earth phosphors (determined by crystal growth), the perovskite's polaron/ion-migration trap hierarchy can be dynamically tuned by changing the excitation wavelength, intensity, and pulse pattern — providing **reconfigurable** synaptic weights absent from the STOKES Yan et al. device.
